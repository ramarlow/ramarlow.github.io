Wisconsin Robotics decided to retire their long-serving rover at the end of the 2024 URC season to make way for a full redesign, so we got permission to display the old one in a corner of the ME building, for which I helped build a small display platform.

The design and color scheme of the display was selected to match the stained oak moulding of the surrounding building and nearby displays. We spent a while trying different combinations of leftover stain and wood scraps in the school's wood shop until we found a convincing match. The lumber was sourced from Home Depot and transported the 5 miles back to campus via a combination of a teammates car, Madison public transit, and taped to the side of my bike.

{% include gallery caption="Rover display CAD model and finished platform" %}

![image-right](/assets/images/rover-info.png){: width="40%" .align-right}
To simulate the rover's natural environment and highlight its neat suspension system we thought it'd be cool to create a patch of sand with height variation and Mars-like rocks. This was done by cutting out layers of foam board and stacking them to form small hills, laying paper mache over the top to smooth the stair-stepping, and finally gluing down a bunch of sand. We considered using some of the competition soil from Utah for authenticity, but decided it was too valuable for testing the [science system](/projects/seniordes). Thankfully the glue coating our fairly pale sand gave it a slightly redder tint that ended up working out well. For the rocks we looked through images of the martian landscape to get a sense of how the average Mars rock looks, then sourced them from a couple local rock piles including rubble from demolition for the new engineering building. Finally I made a little plaque with the team roster from the 2024 season that we put on the side.
